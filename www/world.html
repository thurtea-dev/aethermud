The World

The Great Cataclysm ended humanity's golden age in a single night. The energy of millions of deaths poured back into the earth, and the ley lines, dormant rivers of magic, erupted. Where they cross, nexus points tear open rifts: doorways to a thousand alien worlds, and the nightmares that live in them. Three centuries later, technology and magic contest every mile of a transformed Earth. This is where you come in.

Honest status labels. Zones marked live are built and playable today. in progress zones exist but are small; expect a handful of rooms while they grow. planned zones are not in the game yet. Room counts come from the measured project audit (docs/ASSESSMENT.md), not wishful thinking.

Getting around

Every journey starts in Praxis, the frontier hub where new characters arrive. In the town square you will find Moxim, a rift master who tears open temporary portals between the continents for a fee in universal credits: type rift <destination>, hand over the money, and step through before the portal collapses. Anyone standing nearby can follow you in, so choose your traveling company carefully. A hovertrain links the Chi-Town Burbs with the town of Horton for those who prefer rails to rifts.

The Americas

Praxis live

The starting hub: a rough frontier town grown up around a rift nexus. Bank, market stalls, hospital with a resident cyber-doc, sheriff, library, pubs, a bounty board, and Moxim's rift service. Beyond the town edge lie catacombs, a cemetery, mountain trails, and Coalition territory for those ready to test their gear.

Chi-Town live

The capital of the Coalition States and the largest human city on the continent, a fortified mega-city whose white walls have never been breached. Dog Boys sniff the crowds for psychics, Psi-Stalkers prowl the checkpoints, and SAMAS power armor drones overhead. Outside the walls sprawl the Burbs: shanty towns where the desperate wait for citizenship papers that will never come, and where the Coalition mostly lets everyone fend for themselves. Explore the shopping arcade, the ISS checkpoint plaza, the residential maintenance hatches, and the sewers below, where something many-headed is said to nest. D-bees without forged papers should think very hard before approaching the gate.

Tolkeen live

A free city of magic under Coalition siege. Liberation Square holds its own rift master, and the mage quarter, market, and inn still do brisk trade while CS scouts probe the scorched fields south of the gate. Beneath the square, a warded crypt waits for anyone brave or foolish enough to go down the stairs.

Horton live

A small human town that welcomes anyone, which is rarer than it sounds. Horton sits alone in deep wilderness; apart from days of hard overland travel, the only way in or out is the hovertrain from the Chi-Town Burbs. The forest does not share the town's manners: bandit camps, a flooded quarry with something living on its floor, wolf dens, and a glade where a ley line breaks the surface all lie within a day's walk of Main Street.

Lone Star in progress

The Coalition's genetics and mutant research headquarters, where Dog Boys are made and captured D-bees disappear into the labs. A containment wing with its subjects is built and patrols are heavy; the surrounding complex is still small and actively being expanded. Livable enough, if you are human. If you are not, bring forged papers and an exit plan.

Puerto Angel in progress

A coastal town on the vampire-ruled Mexican coast. Once home to humans, its streets now belong to the undead, who tolerate most visitors but will fight to the death if attacked. Some of them have wandered as far north as the Chi-Town Burbs looking for prey. The docks are working again under a new dockmaster; the beach makes a beautiful resting spot, provided you keep a cross and a canteen of water within reach. Currently a small zone, growing.

Lazlo in progress

The famous city of learning and magic on the shore of old Lake Ontario, where mages, psychics, and D-bees are citizens instead of targets. Only an outpost of it exists in the game so far.

Atlantis

The legendary continent has returned from wherever it went, and it did not come back friendly. Atlantis today belongs to the Splugorth: ancient, tentacled intelligences whose trade empire spans dimensions. Lord Splynncryth rules a land of monstrous denizens, dimensional bazaars, and beautiful horrors. In the mudlib, Splynn, Alvurron, the Preserves, and related Atlantis rooms live under domains/Praxis/areas/splynn/, not as a separate top-level domain.

Splynn live

The capital, and home of the Dimensional Market, where nearly anything can be bought: mega-damage weapons, power armor, magic, and slaves, openly traded in the pens. Rumor says a certain bar owner keeps a talisman worth killing for behind the counter, that knocking on the right pillar opens a blacker market than the public one, and that the Sword of Atlantis lies somewhere beyond the ocean rift. The catacombs under the city hold worse things than rumors.

Alvurron live

The city of the gargoyles. Winged shapes wheel overhead between cavernous dwellings scattered across the rock, and the slave pens do business of their own. Only the brave-of-heart come here, and only the well-armed leave impressed.

The Preserves in progress

Lord Splynncryth's private hunting grounds on the western side of the continent, stocked with ravenous monsters from a dozen worlds and roamed by nomadic tribes of lesser creatures, humans among them. Take a hunting party, pack plenty of e-clips, and remember that on the Preserves you are not necessarily the hunter. The first hunting-grounds loop is open; the deeper preserve is still being built.

Europe

New Camelot live

The court of a returned king, risen in the British Isles with castle, academy, and market street in full ceremony. Perhaps the King himself will make an appearance, flanked by his inhumanly patient advisor. Browse the shops, walk the royal gardens, and be careful what you say among the hedges; some people insist the gardens are alive, and the people who insist loudest no longer walk there.

NGR Germany in progress

What is left of pre-rifts Germany, now held by the New German Republic and armored in Triax engineering, weapons manufacturing that rivals anything on Earth. The NGR fights a grinding war against the gargoyle hordes, and some of its soldiers harbor the same feelings toward D-bees as the Coalition does. A Triax tech pocket is open to visit; connecting wilderness and side villages are on the roadmap.

Elsewhere and otherwise

The demon plane in progress

A sliver of somewhere else entirely, reachable by those who go looking for trouble in dimensional places. Deliberately small, and deliberately unfair.

Stormshire and the Federation of Magic planned

The great city of the magic zone, with wilderness to match, is the next signature destination on the build plan. It is not in the game yet; when it opens, it will be announced rather than quietly oversold.

Africa, Japan, Australia planned

On the long-term map, in that order of ambition. Not yet built.
